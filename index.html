What is the DAL?

A Lab for Creative Bravery

The Digital Art Lab is a resource and inspiration platform for Irish primary teachers who want to bring digital art into their Visual Arts teaching — whether you're just starting out or want fresh ideas to push your practice further.

Digital technology is always at the centre of the DAL. Sometimes you'll start with a screen; sometimes you'll begin with paint or clay and then bring your ideas into a digital tool. What matters is the spirit of experimentation — the willingness to try something, see what happens, and try again.

Everything here has been developed through real workshops with trainee and qualified teachers — experimenting together, taking chances, making mistakes, and discovering what genuinely works in a primary classroom.

Digital First

Apps, drawing tools, animation, photography, video — digital technology is always at the heart of the DAL. Every resource embeds a digital element, whatever direction you take.

Starting Physical, Going Digital

Sometimes the best starting point is a brush, a print, or collage — but the journey always leads into digital territory. Physical materials can spark the idea; technology carries it further.

🔀

Hybrid Practice

Physical and digital processes woven together — responding to each other, building on each other, creating something richer than either could produce alone.

🎯

Always Curriculum-Linked

Whatever the approach, all DAL resources connect to the Irish Primary Curriculum — Visual Arts strands, competencies, and class levels clearly signposted.

Our Principles

What the DAL Stands For

Three beliefs shape everything we make and share on this platform.

01
🧪

Experimentation Over Perfection

The best learning happens when teachers feel free to try things without fear of failure. The process of experimenting is itself the point — not arriving at a polished final product.

02
🎓

Teacher Empowerment

Digital empowerment isn't just about knowing how to use a tool. It's about having the confidence, the curiosity, and the creative purpose to make your own decisions about technology in your teaching.

03
📚

Grounded in the Curriculum

Everything in the DAL connects to the new Irish Primary Curriculum — particularly its emphasis on creativity, agency, inclusion, and contemporary competencies in Visual Arts.

How the DAL Works

From Workshop to Your Classroom

Everything on this platform starts in a real workshop and ends in your hands.

01

We Experiment Together

Trainee and qualified teachers come into the DAL — a real creative studio — to try things out and see what happens with digital art approaches.

02

We Document Everything

Photos, videos, teacher reflections — every step is captured. Not just the finished work, but the thinking, the stumbles, and the unexpected discoveries.

03

We Turn It Into Resources

Workshop experiments are shaped into classroom-ready materials — lesson ideas, tool guides, exemplars, and curriculum links — free to use and adapt.

04

You Take It Further

You bring resources into your own classroom, adapt them, and share what you discover. Your feedback shapes what the DAL develops next.

The Research Behind the DAL

Grounded in Design-Based Research

The DAL is the central practical output of a PhD study at DCU: Fostering Digital Empowerment — An Online Multi-Media Art Resource for Irish Primary School Teachers.

The platform is built iteratively — through real cycles of design, workshop implementation, reflection, and refinement. It is both a research intervention and a practical tool for teachers.

The research sits within the context of the new Irish Primary Curriculum — particularly its emphasis on creativity, agency, inclusion, and contemporary competencies in Visual Arts education.

A PhD Research Project at Dublin City University

The Digital Art Lab is the central output of a funded PhD study at DCU's Institute of Education — one of Ireland's leading centres for educational research and teacher education.

The research is situated within DCU's Faculty of Education, reflecting the university's commitment to purposeful, innovative, and socially engaged scholarship in teaching and learning.
